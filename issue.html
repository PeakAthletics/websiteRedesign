The Issue

The impacts of traumatic events on physicians' mental health are devastating. Almost 25% of physicians self-reported being clinically depressed. One in eleven physicians have suicidal thoughts. Unfortunately, physicians have the highest suicide rate of any profession, with a rate more than 2x the general population. In addition to developing mental health illnesses, a study classified sixty percent of physicians as suffering from burnout.
This has ripple effects throughout society. Cross-sectional studies recognize that if a physician is experiencing burnout, there is a correlation to lower performance: twice the risk of medical error and a 17% increase in the likelihood of a malpractice lawsuit.
There is a lack of adequate mental health resources offered to physicians both within the hospital and on the market. Current wellness programs require participation outside of work hours, focus on yoga and resilience podcasts, and do not acknowledge the night shift schedule of many physicians. Often, trauma happens in the middle of the night, when peers or therapists are not present, and there are more patients to see in a physician's rounds. Moreover, current therapy apps do not leverage peer support along with therapist expertise. Physicians often feel that only their colleagues are in the unique position to understand their trauma. Due to malpractice lawsuits and fear of discoverability, some trauma can only be shared with a therapist*.
Thus, an effective support system must offer both peer and therapist support. This is why we created WYN Health.
